üéØ What is Command of the Message?

Command of the Message is a strategic framework developed by Force Management that helps organizations create a unified, customer-centric approach to sales and marketing. It ensures everyone in your company communicates consistent value and differentiation.

Core Principle: Move conversations away from your products and features, and toward the customer's business problems and desired outcomes.

Why It Matters

  • Alignment: Sales, marketing, and customer success all speak the same language
  • Differentiation: Stand out in crowded markets with value-based positioning
  • Win Rates: Higher conversion when you lead with customer outcomes
  • Deal Velocity: Faster sales cycles when messaging resonates
  • Predictability: Consistent messaging creates repeatable results

üìä The Three Levels of Conversation

Command of the Message teaches you to elevate conversations through three progressive levels:

Level 1: Products & Features

What you sell: "Our platform has AI-powered analytics and real-time dashboards."

‚ùå Problem: Commoditizes your solution. Anyone can claim features.

Level 2: Solutions & Benefits

What it does: "Our platform helps you make faster decisions with better visibility."

‚ö†Ô∏è Better, but: Still focused on you, not their specific problems.

Level 3: Business Value & Impact Target

What it means to them: "Companies like yours reduce forecast variance by 40% and close deals 25% faster, giving CFOs the predictability they need for board meetings."

‚úì Best: Speaks to their world, their metrics, their stakeholders.

The Goal: Spend 80% of your conversations at Level 3. This is where deals are won and lost.

üíé The Value Wedge

The Value Wedge is your core message architecture. It answers three critical questions:

1. Required Capabilities

What must your solution do?

  • The table stakes - what any solution in your category needs
  • Usually 3-5 core capabilities
  • Example: "Real-time data integration, predictive analytics, role-based access"

2. Differentiating Capabilities

What makes you unique?

  • The 2-3 things you do that competitors can't match
  • Must be provable and defendable
  • Example: "Only solution with bi-directional CRM sync that preserves all historical data"

3. Value Drivers

Why does it matter to their business?

  • The business outcomes your capabilities enable
  • Quantified impact on revenue, cost, risk, or efficiency
  • Example: "40% reduction in sales forecasting errors, leading to $2M+ in prevented missed opportunities"
Critical Connection: Your differentiators must connect directly to measurable value drivers. If a differentiator doesn't drive quantifiable business value, it's not a real differentiator.

üîç Discovery: Uncover Before You Cover

Command of the Message emphasizes that you must discover before you present. The discovery process follows a structured path:

The Discovery Sequence

  1. Current State: What's happening today? What's working? What's not?
  2. Problems: What specific challenges are you facing?
  3. Impact: What's the business impact of those problems?
  4. Root Cause: Why do these problems exist?
  5. Decision Criteria: What would an ideal solution look like?
  6. Required Outcomes: What results would make this a success?
Common Mistake: Jumping to solution mode too early. Resist the urge to pitch. Ask more questions, especially about business impact and decision criteria.

Power Questions

  • "What prompted you to look at solving this now?"
  • "What's the cost of not solving this?"
  • "Who else is impacted by this problem?"
  • "What have you tried before? What worked? What didn't?"
  • "What would success look like 12 months from now?"
  • "What are the consequences if we can't solve this?"

üéÅ Positive Business Outcomes (PBOs)

PBOs are the measurable, desirable results your customers achieve. They're the "so what?" of your solution.

Structure of a Strong PBO

[Quantified Metric] + [Timeframe] + [Business Context]

Example: "Reduce sales cycle length by 30% within 6 months, enabling the sales team to close 15+ more deals per quarter and exceed revenue targets."

Categories of PBOs

üí∞ Revenue Growth

Increase sales, improve win rates, expand deal sizes, accelerate pipeline velocity

üí∏ Cost Reduction

Lower operational costs, reduce waste, optimize resource allocation

‚ö° Efficiency Gains

Save time, automate processes, eliminate manual work

üõ°Ô∏è Risk Mitigation

Reduce compliance exposure, improve security, prevent errors

Best Practice: Have 3-5 core PBOs that you can prove with customer data and case studies. Make them specific to different buyer personas.

üèóÔ∏è Building Your Command of the Message

Step-by-Step Process

Step 1: Customer Research

Interview 10-15 customers to understand:

  • What problems prompted them to buy
  • What outcomes they've achieved
  • What would have happened if they didn't buy
  • How they describe the value to others

Step 2: Define Value Wedge

Based on research, document:

  • 3-5 Required Capabilities (table stakes)
  • 2-3 Differentiating Capabilities (unique)
  • 4-6 Value Drivers (quantified PBOs)

Step 3: Create Proof Points

For each value driver, gather:

  • Customer case studies with metrics
  • Before/after data
  • Industry benchmarks
  • ROI calculations

Step 4: Persona Mapping

Customize messaging for each buyer:

  • CFO: Cost reduction, ROI, risk mitigation
  • CRO: Revenue growth, win rates, efficiency
  • COO: Process optimization, scalability
  • End Users: Ease of use, time savings

Step 5: Sales Enablement

Arm your team with:

  • Discovery question guides
  • Value wedge presentation deck
  • Objection handling scripts
  • ROI calculator templates

Step 6: Continuous Refinement

Evolve your message by:

  • Tracking win/loss reasons
  • Gathering competitive intelligence
  • Updating proof points quarterly
  • A/B testing different value props

üí° Implementation Best Practices

Getting Started

  • Start with Why: Don't mandate the framework - show the team how it makes their lives easier
  • Lead with Leadership: Executives must use the messaging first
  • Train with Role Play: Practice elevating from Level 1 to Level 3 in real scenarios
  • Integrate Everywhere: Use in proposals, decks, website, case studies, demos
  • Certify Your Team: Test understanding before letting reps use it with customers

Common Pitfalls to Avoid

  • Over-Complication: Keep the Value Wedge simple - 3x3x5 max
  • Inside-Out Thinking: Validate everything with customers, not internal opinions
  • Weak Differentiation: "Better, faster, easier" is not differentiation
  • No Proof: Every value claim needs customer evidence
  • One-Size-Fits-All: Customize by persona, industry, deal size

Measuring Success

Leading Indicators

  • % of discovery calls at Level 3
  • Win rate in competitive situations
  • Average discount given
  • Time to first meeting

Lagging Indicators

  • Overall win rate improvement
  • Average deal size growth
  • Sales cycle reduction
  • Customer retention rates

That means you hit quota faster with the same headcount, and your CFO sees ROI in half the time."
